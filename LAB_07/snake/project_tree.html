Design hierarchy — instantiated inside Snake_Top

For the synthesizable design (what runs on the FPGA) every module is instantiated once inside Snake_Top. The hierarchy is mostly flat — Snake_Top instantiates all the blocks directly — with one exception: Food_Planter is itself a small subsystem, shown nested one level deeper below (and detailed in the next section).

top level new (write it) adapt reuse (import)

Inside Food_Planter (subsystem)

Food_Planter is not one block — it is a small hierarchy of its own. It uses a pseudo-random generator to propose a candidate cell and rejects any candidate that lands on the snake's body, looping until it finds a free cell, then latches the food position.

How it picks a free cell (internal dataflow)

On a food-eaten (or reset) pulse, Spawn_Ctrl keeps pulling fresh pseudo-random candidates from the LFSR and rejecting any that Body_Check reports as on the snake — until it finds a free cell, which it latches as food_x, food_y. Since the LFSR runs at 100 MHz, a free cell is found in nanoseconds. (You can split LFSR into per-axis LFSR_X / LFSR_Y if you prefer two generators.)

Simulation hierarchy — where each module is instantiated in a testbench

A testbench has no ports: it is the top of its own simulation. It generates clk, reset and stimulus, and instantiates the module under test (DUT). You write one small unit-tb per new / tricky module, plus one full-system tb that instantiates Snake_Top.

Reused modules come with tests: Ps2_Interface_tb and VGA_Interface_tb are ready-made from Labs 5 & 6; Seg_7_Display and Debouncer are already proven, so they don't need new unit testbenches.
